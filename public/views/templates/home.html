Why is this important?

If I care about water in the State (and my water bill), then I should have a basic understanding of whether my bill is fair, or my water supply is sustainable. We do not worry about where our coffee comes from, or care, but we DO need to know about our water supply. Our water has been reliable for decades, but underinvestment and climate change mean our systems are more vulnerable to shocks. Bad luck also counts: that's what people in Charleston, WV found out when chemicals got in their water. Californians have had water shortages and pollution before, and we should try to avoid those problems in the future.

What outcomes might this project inspire?

Your curiosity will help everyone, since data on water prices and water use helps us understand where water services and supplies are at risk. Understanding can move people to act much more quickly to reduce the risks.

Who is this project for?

Anyone interested in community water supply, sustainability, or service. Customers have the most to gain from information, but most of them are too busy to collect or analyze it. Crowdsourced data can help the Atlas present more information in a clearer form, so that everyone can understand what's happening with water in our State.
